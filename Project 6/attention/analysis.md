# Analysis

## Layer 4, Head 1

The head seems to focus on words with the same or similar meaning, so duplicates or synonyms.

Example Sentences:
- I saw a book, a novel by a person who wrote [MASK]
- A happy, delighted man met a happy, delighted, [MASK] person.
- a b a b a [MASK] a

## Layer 6, Head 8

This head focuses on relations between verbs and adjectives. but it only focuses on the adjective that is directly related to the verb. For example, in the sentence "He met Holmes, a cunning [MASK]." The adjective "cunning" is not directly related to the verb "met", so the head doesn't focus on it, but in the sentence "This is a concerning [MASK]", the adjective "concerning" is directly related to the verb "is", so the head focuses on it.

Example Sentences:
- This is a concerning [MASK].
- That idea was funny [MASK] peculiar.
